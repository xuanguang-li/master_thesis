\section{Conclusion}
\label{sec:conclusion}
This paper revisits the statement of \cite{justinianoInvestmentShocksBusiness2010}, who claims that the investment specific technology is the main driving force of the business cycle, by testing an alternative hypothesis - investment demand shock proposed by \cite{nireiOriginsPropagationAnimal}. Building a DSGE model with both IST shock and investment demand shock and estimating it using Bayesian methods, the results show that investment demand shock can be another competing shock driving the business cycle. Investment demand shock alone contributes about 40\% of the forecast error of output, and about 50\% of investment.

To address the concerns of the identification problem and oversimplification of the model specification, Section \ref{sec:robustness} provides two robustness tests. One uses the relative price data to further identify IST shock, and the other uses a larger model similar to \cite{smetsShocksFrictionsUS2007} to solidify the results estimated from the baseline model.

Overall, investment demand shock can be a promising candidate to explain the business cycle along with its theoretical ability to generate the comovements between various macroeconomic variables. This paper provides empirical support in a representative-agent DSGE model. However, this problem can still be revisited in a more theoretically grounded heterogeneous-agent model.
