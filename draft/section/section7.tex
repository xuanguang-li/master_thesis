\section{Conclusion}
\label{sec:conclusion}
This paper revisits \cite{justinianoInvestmentShocksBusiness2010}, who claims that the investment specific technology is the main driving force of the business cycle, by testing an alternative hypothesis---investment demand shock proposed by \cite{nireiOriginsPropagationAnimal}. Building a DSGE model with both IST shock and investment demand shock, the estimation results show that investment demand shock can be another competing shock driving the business cycle. In the baseline result, investment demand shock alone contributes about 40\% of the forecast error of output, and about 50\% of investment.

However, there are few limitations in this paper. First, there lacks data to directly identify invesetment demand shock, and thus the identifiability of invesetment demand shock and IST shock can only be based on their different effect on other state variables. However, it is hard to test the argument among universal combinations of parameter values, as the parameter dimension is high. Section~\ref{sec:results:inf} and \ref{sec:results:con} can only provide partial evidence on this argument.
Second, baseline model cannot fit the data very well, and the posteriors of some parameters are extreme values, which is disccused in Section \ref{sec:estimation:dist} and \ref{sec:results:moments}. Third, estimation results depend on model specification. Expanding the model (Section~\ref{sec:robustness:sw}) or using alternative stochastic process (Section~\ref{sec:robustness:iid}) can alter the result quite a lot. 

Nevertheless, this paper highlights the insights provided by structural estimation, but also the fragility of such approach as well. More macroeconomic effect of invesetment-side shocks can be discussed and compared, but the selection of model specification should be careful.
